The Semantics of “Pre-U”

October 21, 2014November 12, 2014Daniel Wen

I underwent a brief moment of epiphany when I discovered the semantics of using the name “Pre-University” over “Grade 12.” When viewed as a preparation for university instead of merely a final year of high school, LCC’s grandfathered programme has genuine reasons for its otherwise quirky characteristics. As students, we must forgo comfort and commit ourselves to the rigorous training.


Our class schedules resemble—artificially, I suspect—the relatively less rigid timetables in university. The lunch breaks are converted into unscheduled periods and scattered haphazardly throughout the day. As a result, we may eat in class. The novelty soon becomes inconvenience as we struggle to simultaneously pack our brains and our mouths. Still, this allows some flexible use of our spare periods and requires some planning to avoid ingesting anything during chemistry. In addition, our classes are unevenly distributed over the two-week cycle such that on some days, we must toil without respite for seven hours, and on others, we can sleep in till noon. However, despite the hectic work and the disappearance of lunchtime, we still have recess every day at 10:35. Yay!

Again, at the expense of comfort, we wear business attire in Pre-U in anticipation of U and Post-U life. We are reassured that we look “professional” and not supercilious in the least. Professional students definitely exist outside of high school; you can find them, for example, in internships, co-op programs, and in Denmark. Such students are our role models, and while we currently acknowledge that we are merely amateurs, we refine our wardrobes knowing we will one day be professionals. We have also been instructed to keep up the “corporateness” of the institution through careful clothing. I think the adjective “corporate” is aptly associated with people dressed in suits, especially when paired with nouns to form juxtapositions such as “corporate crime.”

Finally, now that we are the elders of the student body, we are indulged with privileges. We are free to operate the elevator, use a laptop our choice, and take advantage of the Front Door.
